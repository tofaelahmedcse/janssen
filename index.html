It's time
to give patients more time

It is widely accepted that a diagnosis of mCRPC means a poor prognosis1

Patients with metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC) face:

  • • Median survival less than 3 years
  • • Severe symptoms including bone pain and other skeletal-related events
  • • Significant deterioration in quality of life

Before progressing from nmCRPC to mCRPC, patients are usually asymptomatic and have a better quality of life2

Patients with non-metastatic CRPC (nmCRPC) tend to have good:

  • • Vitality
  • • Social function
  • • Mental health.

ADT and CAB are most commonly used treatments in nmCRPC3

Many clinicians in Asia Pacific use traditional treatments such as androgen deprivation therapy (ADT) and conventional combined androgen blockage (CAB)

But the data to support the role of ADT and CAB is suboptimal4

ADT alone leads to disease progression to mCRPC in 1–2 years.

The clinical efficacy of CAB is also limited, with no significant difference in survival seen between CAB

Clinical guidelines reflect the limited clinical benefit of ADT and CAB in nmCRPC5

Several key international guidelines have been updated to reflect the limited clinical benefit of using CAB and ADT alone in patients with nmCRPC.

Strong data supports the role of novel hormone therapies, in addition to ADT, early in the treatment of nmCRPC6

e.g. apalutamide + ADT:

Pivotal clinical trials in patients with nmCRPC showed:

  • • 25% reduction in risk of death compared with placebo + ADT (P=0.0197)1
  • • Two additional years without metastasis Metastasis-free survival (MFS) of 40.5 months v.s 16.2 months with placebo + ADT (P<0.001)2*
  • • One additional year without a second progression Second progression free survival (PFS2) extended by 11.8 months versus placebo + ADT (apalutamide + ADT 55.6 months versus placebo + ADT 43.8 months)**
  • • 45% reduction in risk of secondary progression or death compared with placebo + ADT after the development of mCRPC (P<0.0001).

Experts agree the treatment goals for nmCRPC patients are to delay metastases and maintain quality of life

It’s time

to change the way we treat patients advanced prostate cancer

It’s time

to recognize the body of evidence and put it into practice. CAB and ADT are no longer enough in the treatment of nmCRPC.

It’s time

to intervene early, using a novel hormone therapy, to give your patients with nmCRPC more time to enjoy life with their family and friends.
